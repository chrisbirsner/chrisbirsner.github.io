Arthritis Foundation Races To $50,000

By Chris Birsner & Jessica Jahn

The Arthritis Foundation's Long Island Chapter hosted their tenth annual Jingle Bell Run to raise awareness for one of the most common disabilities in America. It's one of many events happening around the country. They raised over $50,000 for their services.

Runners weren't only getting into the holiday spirit. Many were there to raise awareness and show support for family and friends who are suffering from the disease.

What many people fail to realize is that arthritis affects infants and children as well.

Arthritis is an inflammation of the joints that causes pain, swelling, and stiffness. The pain isn't always constant as it comes and goes randomly. As of now, there is no cure.

Over 50 million adults are affected by arthrtis, according to the Arthrtis Foundation. That is one in every four individuals over the age of 18.

Robin Pierre, the Arthritis Foundation's development manager on Long Island, has worked with the foundation for 32 years and has been in charge of the Jingle Bell Run for the last four.

“There’s been a support system," Pierre said, "What we’ve done in advocacy has been wonderful. We like getting our faces out there. I think what we do for the younger population is great as well; Bringing it more to the forefront.”

The Arthritis Foundation has four pillars: Juvenile Arthritis, Help & Support, Research, and Advocacy. Events like the Jingle Bell Run, as well as Walk To Cure Arthritis in the spring, allow the organization to keep these four pillars standing.

Pierre encourages those interested in helping the cause to get involved.

“Come to the events, participate in the events. We always need volunteers because of health limitation," Pierre said, "it’s imperative that we have volunteers. Become an advocate. You don’t need to have arthritis to be an advocate. Check out our website on arthritis.org.”
